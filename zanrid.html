Genres Of Video Games

Video game genre - specific category of games related by similar gameplay characteristics.
Video game genres are usually defined by the way the player interacts with the game.
Sometimes video games combine elements of several genres.
Genres may have subgenres.

This section describes only 8 largest genres (and does not include subgenres).
For more detailed information visit this website.

Action

Emphasize physical challenges
Center around the player
Examples: Middle-Earth: Shadow Of War, Red Dead Redemption, Cyberpunk 2077

Adventure

No reflex challenges or action Require to solve various puzzles
Require to interact with people or the environment
Examples: Life Is Strange, A Way Out, Ghostrunner

Action-adventure

Feature long-term and small obstacles
Focus on exploration
Involve item gathering, simple puzzle solving, combat
Examples: The Last Of Us, The Rise Of The Tomb Raider, Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order

Role-playing

Player is a weak character that grows in strength and experience
Require overcoming difficult challenges and/or defeating monsters
Player gains experience points which represent the character's progress in a chosen profession or class
Player can gain new abilities
Examples: Mass Effect, The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim, Diablo III

Simulation

Closely simulate aspects of a real or fictional reality
Examples: Spore, Kerbal Space Program, The Sims 4

Strategy

Require careful and skillful thinking and planning
Action scales from world domination to squad-based tactics
Examples: Plants vs. Zombies, XCOM 2, Crusader Kings 3

Sports

Simulate sports
Opposing team(s) can be controlled by other real life people or artificial intelligence
Examples: Tony Hawk's Pro Skater2, Wii Fit, FIFA 18

MMO/MMOG

Massively multiplayer online game
Capable of supporting large numbers of players simultaneously
Played on the Internet
Enable players to cooperate and compete with each other
Include a variety of gameplay types and many video game genres
Examples: EVE Online, World Of Warcraft, Darkfall
