A Little About Video Games' History 🕹️

A video game is an electronic game that involves interaction with a user interface or
input device to generate visual feedback for a player.

Then it is shown on a 2D or 3D video display device or virtual reality headset.
Video games are augmented with audio feedback and optionally with other types of feedback systems.
Video games are defined based on their platform - arcade games, console games and PC games.
More recently, the industry has expanded onto mobile gaming.

Video games are classified into a wide range of genres based on their type of gameplay and purpose.

First Video Game And It's Developer

The world’s first video game is Tennis For Two
It was the first computer game to be created purely as an entertainment product
rather than for academic research or commercial technology promotion

It was created by physicist William Higinbotham and shown on October 18, 1958.

Evolution Of Video Games

As the time passed by and technologies developed the video games changed too.
Best way to show it is to compare some older video games with some new ones.

1973. Space Race

Space Race was the second game by the
company Atari, Inc., after Pong (1972), which marked
the beginning of the commercial video game industry

1980. Pac-Man

The Pac-Man video game franchise remains one of the
highest-grossing and best-selling game series of all time,
generating more than $14 billion in revenue (as of 2016) and
$43 million in sales combined.

1990. Super Mario World

Super Mario World was produced during the console wars
– a result of the rivalry between Nintendo's SNES and
Sega's two-year-old Mega Drive system.
Nintendo has sold 20.61 million copies of the game
worldwide, making it the bestselling game for the SNES.

2000. Banjo Tooie

The graphics were considered some of the best
on the Nintendo 64 due to their rich textures,
long drawing distance, and real-time shadow generation,
but were criticised for their inconsistent frame rate during
certain points in the game.

2010. God Of War III

Upon release, God of War III received countless positive
review scores with critics praising the game’s brutal
combat, impressive visuals and grand scale.
In his review of the game, Joe Juba of website
Gameinformer gave the game a perfect 10 out of 10, and
concluded his article by saying that
“Not even in my wildest dreams could I have imagined such
a powerful, cinematic, and breathtaking conclusion to the
saga of the Ghost of Sparta.”

2020. Assassin's Creed: Valhalla

Assassin’s Creed Valhalla would undoubtedly benefit from
Nvidia’s DLSS technology which uses machine learning to
improve image quality and performance.
Valhalla represents Ubisoft's "flagship" game for
these next-generation systems, and has been developed to take
advantage of faster loading times both new consoles offer.
Players on Xbox One or PlayStation 4 are able to upgrade their game to
the next-gen version on their respective platform at no extra cost.
